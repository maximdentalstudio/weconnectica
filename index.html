Even the best teams struggle when culture gets overlooked

In multicultural teams, misunderstandings don’t just happen once in a while, they’re part of daily life. And if left unchecked, they can quietly erode trust, performance, and retention.

It’s more than an inconvenience:

  • One key hire leaving in the first year can cost €50–100K
  • Decisions slow down, rework piles up, and onboarding takes longer
  • High performers can feel isolated, leading to quiet quitting
  • Engagement drops and collaboration suffers

Communication = success

Research shows that in almost every case, cultural challenges — not professional skill gaps — are the main reason international hires leave early.

Ineffective communication costs companies up to $1.2 trillion annually*. According to The Harris Poll’s report:

In 99% of cases, cultural issues rather than lack of professional competence are the reason why expatriates cut short their foreign business trips.*

“Expatriate Management: A Review and Directions for Research in Expatriate Selection, Training, and Repatriation”
